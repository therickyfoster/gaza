Executive Overview

Intent: Connect communities of faith like a living mycelial network—quiet threads, shared nourishment, distinct blossoms. The design very gently and extrasuperficially hints toward a merger of efforts (service, learning, care) while protecting each tradition’s identity. Think federation: many roots, one shared canopy of care.

Metaphor

Mycelium moves nutrients across species without asking leaves to change shape. Collaboration ≠ conversion.

Practice

Joint service, shared calendars, cross-ritual hospitality, sacred ecology, trauma-aware dialogue.

Guardrails

Freedom of conscience, consent, informed boundaries, opt-out at any time, no pressure tactics.

Mycelial Principles • Roots, Threads, Fruiting Bodies

Roots (Identity)

  • Texts, rites, and lineages remain intact.
  • Names, symbols, languages honored without dilution.
  • Right to difference is sacred and explicit.

Threads (Exchange)

  • Share surplus: kitchens, halls, buses, volunteers, tools.
  • Cross-train practical skills: mediation, first aid, grief care.
  • Resource commons: libraries, seed banks, mutual aid.

Fruiting (Public Work)

  • Joint service days (shelter, food, river cleanups).
  • Interfaith apprenticeships & chaplaincy co-placements.
  • Festival hospitality exchanges & shared art.
Collaboration Modules • Gentle Convergence

Ritual Reciprocity (opt-in)

  • “Open Door” hours: quiet observation without obligation.
  • Blessing exchanges: leaders offer a neutral well-wishing.
  • Calendar braiding: public events avoid conflicts; occasional co-hosting.

Service Weave

  • Food & water drives, elder visits, hospital rounds.
  • Climate & sacred ecology: tree plantings, watershed care.
  • Emergency mutual aid: sheltering, logistics, chaplaincy.

Learning Commons

  • Trauma-aware dialogue circles with certified facilitators.
  • Language & song exchanges; shared arts nights.
  • Shared ethics seminars on compassion, justice, stewardship.

Mycelial Kitchen (hospitality)

  • Rotating interfaith community meals (dietary laws respected).
  • Volunteer swapboard; accessible transport pooling.
  • Shared hygiene & care packs for unhoused neighbors.
Safeguards • Consent, Boundaries, Equity

Consent Protocol

  • Triple consent: personal, family/guardian where applicable, and community lead.
  • Clear “no questions” opt-out; no tracking beyond what’s needed for safety.
  • Ritual spaces: observe dress/food rules; secure storage for sacred objects.

No-Pressure Policy

  • No proselytizing inside joint events.
  • No bait-and-switch invitations.
  • Report & remedy channel with restorative steps.

The “merger” here is a merger of service, not a merger of creeds. We hint with gestures—shared calendars, joint signatures on service outcomes—while protecting theological diversity.

180-Day Roadmap • From Threads to Canopy

Days 0–30

  • Interfaith pledge & code of conduct; consent templates.
  • Shared calendar alpha; hospitality exchange roster.
  • Facilitator training (trauma-aware, de-escalation, grief care).

Days 31–180

  • Monthly service days; quarterly festival hospitality.
  • Dialogue circles; chaplaincy co-placements (opt-in).
  • Open dashboard of outcomes; independent ombud review.
